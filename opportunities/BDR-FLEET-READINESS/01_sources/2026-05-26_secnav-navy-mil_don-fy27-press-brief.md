## Summary

Department of the Navy FY 2027 President's Budget Press Brief, dated 28 April 2026. 17-page slide deck signed at the policy level by Acting Secretary of the Navy Hung Cao. Total FY27 DON budget is $377.5B ($338.7B discretionary + $38.8B mandatory). The headline numbers are $150.0B Procurement (+45% over FY26), $111.2B Operation & Maintenance (+20%), $34.4B Aviation Procurement (+93%), $22.6B Weapons Procurement (+123%), and $65.8B Shipbuilding & Conversion Navy (+46%). Ship Maintenance is funded at $17.0B (including OPN) and drives the fleet toward an "80% Combat Surge Ready (CSR) posture." Maritime Industrial Base investments total $7.0B in FY27, including $1.8B for Shipyard Infrastructure Optimization Program (SIOP). The Golden Fleet Initiative is the policy umbrella for the distributed-shipbuilding industrial-base push.

For the BDR-FLEET-READINESS scope, the key load-bearing claims are: (a) Combat Surge Ready posture is named explicitly as a metric ("80% CSR"); (b) Contested Logistics is funded at $0.6B specifically to address adversary targeting of supply lines, ports, and communications — directly adjacent to the operational-decision-scenario scope; (c) the 37 Forward Deployed Naval Forces number anchors the forward-deployed fleet that BDAR/BDAT teams have to service.

### Page 3 — PB27 Supports Enduring Priorities

PB27 makes generational investments in the Fleet of the Future, while continuing to invest in today's readiness and our Sailors and Marines.

**Generational Investment in the Maritime Arsenal of Freedom**
- Invests in a high-low mix of platforms to implement the Hedge Strategy with scalable options
- Changing our business by empowering Portfolio Acquisition Executives and establishing the Rapid Capabilities Office to accelerate cutting-edge systems
- Leverages new acquisition strategies to procure 123 aircraft, 4,612 munitions and doubles the investments in aviation spares and repair parts
- Accelerates Force Design

**Rebuild Our Military**
- Rebuilds the Nation's Foundry for the Golden Fleet Initiative by implementing a distributed shipbuilding model, revitalizing the industry to tap into nationwide capacity, strengthening supply chain resilience, and bolstering high-skilled American jobs
- Invests in BBG(X) for a more capable and lethal force to win the Fight and FF(X) Frigate to relieve DDG 51 of lower-priority tasking and enable the Hedge Strategy

**Defend the Homeland and Deter Our Adversaries**
- Procures 34 ships (18 battle force) including 1 Columbia, 2 Virginia Submarines, and 8 Amphibious Ships

**Investing in America's Sons and Daughters**
- People are our asymmetric advantage and the foundation of our readiness
- Invests in Quality of Service for unaccompanied housing and Barracks 2030, along with restoration projects for critical infrastructure
- Increases our Sailors and Marines total compensation and grows our force
- Enhancements for Sailor and Marine individual and team training
- Historic investments in our shipyards and warfighting infrastructure

### Page 5 — Changing How We Do Business

> "We cannot solve the problems of tomorrow with the solutions of yesterday." — Acting SECNAV Cao

- **Audit:** Building on USMC success for all of the Department of the Navy
- **Restoring Shipbuilding Discipline & Industrial Stability:** Balancing demanding contracts with incentives to achieve predictable, on-time delivery and shared outcomes
- **Empowering Command Accountability In Acquisition:** Establishing Portfolio Acquisition Executives (PAEs) as single, accountable commanders to enable trade-offs and ensure swift delivery of lethal capabilities
- **Accelerating Delivery through Innovation:** Establishing the Department of the Navy Rapid Capabilities Office to eliminate bureaucracy and serve as a single entity for prototyping and delivering scalable capabilities to the warfighter
- **Leveraging Private Capital:** Creating new acquisition pathways by engaging the private sector to better balance opportunity and risk
- **Requirements & Budget Reform:** Accelerating timelines for requirements process; reinvesting $8.7B of savings into warfighting priorities
