A. Mission Description and Budget Item Justification
Joint C5 Capability Development, Integration, and Interoperability Assessments program leads Command, Control, Communications, Computers, and Cyber (C5)
assessments, analyses, capability development, and Joint Fires Command and Control (C2) interoperability efforts required to achieve an effective, integrated, and
interoperable combined joint force.  Efforts include C5 requirements determination, architectures development and integration, data and standards, integration, Allies
and coalition integration and interoperability assessments, and Joint Fires C2 interoperability coordinating firepower compatibility.
The Joint Staff leads the Department of War’s effort to develop and integrate the Combined Joint All-Domain Command and Control (CJADC2) across the Department.
The Joint C5 program is the Joint Staff focal point for this responsibility.  Its projects provide the research, analysis, and execution needed to make the CJADC2 strategy
a reality.

A. Mission Description and Budget Item Justification
Project 002 Capability Development. This project functions as the Department of War requirements lead for the joint command and control (C2), cyber, and mission
partner environment. It focuses on creating common architectures and data standards to improve data access and interoperability. C5 development enables warfighter
access to data sources and improves data interoperability by establishing common command and control (C2) data and standards.
B. Accomplishments/Planned Programs ($ in Millions) FY 2025 FY 2026 FY 2027
Title: C5 Capability Development
Description: Lead C2 capability development and integration to achieve an interdependent joint force.  Supports Chairman's
focus to improve joint warfighter capability and enhance global integration. This will be accomplished through five focus areas:
capability development, C5 architectures, data and services, coalition interoperability, and joint experimentation.
FY 2026 Plans:
Capability Development - Provides the Joint Force with analysis and requirements integration support to continuously modernize
C2 for the Joint Force to enable Globally Integrated Operations.  Through Joint All-Domain Command and Control (CJADC2)
Doctrine, Organization, Training, Material, Leadership, Personnel, Facilities and Policy capability requirements identification,
verification, prioritization, and management, Joint Staff J6 is the Department of War’s (DoW’s) capability sponsor for CJADC2
and works with Services, Combatant Commands, and Agencies to identify, synchronize, and plan modernization of Joint C2
solutions through concept development, fielding, and sustainment. Critical activities include analysis and management of C2
capability gaps across the joint force through the annual Capability Gap Analysis Process, continuous CJADC2 capability
requirements management as Requirements lead for the integrated DoW-wide Capability Portfolio Management, and an annual
Capability Portfolio Management Review. Capability Needs capture database management and analysis. The Joint Staff, J6
oversees an agile requirements validation and traceability process that greatly reduces time for joint capability developers to
deliver and integrate and sustain solutions and programs. Additional key activities for J6 include conducting DoW-wide studies
on C2, support to integration of emerging command and control joint doctrine, and assisting the DoW, and others with rapidly
transitioning capability to Combatant Commands and into Service or Joint programs. Key sponsor for the development and
maturity of CJADC2 Portfolio Capability Management tools to support alignment of capabilities to warfighter missions. Leads the
Combined Communications-Electronic Boards’s Future Working Group to ensure interoperability among the Five Eyes (FVEY)
Nations.

A. Mission Description and Budget Item Justification
The Joint Staff Analytical Support (JSAS) program provides analytic and management support to the Chairman’s Title 10 requirement to develop urgent and long-term
Joint concepts. These threat-based Joint concepts advance Joint force operational effectiveness and enable the introduction and incorporation of new capabilities. The
Joint concepts program supports the Chairman's responsibility to articulate a vision for the future Joint force; address operational problems on a 20-year horizon; identify
Joint force development implications; and identify capabilities required to mitigate and solve future Joint warfighting gaps. The goal is to enable investment decisions
balancing near and long-term risk. Threat-informed Joint concepts drive capability development and promote horizontal integration for force development across the
Services, Combatant Commands, Defense agencies, OSW and Joint Staff. Key deliverables include:
Joint Operating Environment (JOE) and the Gamechangers report: Developed in partnership with the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA), these reports describe
the future security environment and project the implications of change for the Joint Force. The documents describe the circumstances that may alter the security
environment and explore how the intersection and interaction of these changes might impact the character of war in the future. They provide a framework to think about
the full range of Joint force missions and how they may evolve over time to support development of threat-based future Joint concepts and CONOPS.
Joint Warfighting Design 2040 (JWD 2040): Describes the attributes of the future Joint force and identifies areas for Service examination and experimentation. It
prepares the Joint force for the future character of warfare – as outlined in the Joint Operating Environment – and provides a force design that balances unity of effort
with Service-specific contributions. JWD 2040 is developed through ongoing collaboration with the Services, Combatant Commands, Defense Agencies, the Intelligence
Community, industry, academia, and key allies and partners.
The Joint Warfighting Concept (JWC):  The Department of War's overarching vision for how the Joint Force will fight in the future. It provides a threat-informed,
operational concept for how the U.S. military will operate as a cohesive team across all domains, including land, sea, air, space, and cyberspace. The JWC is designed
to guide the development of future force structure, capabilities, and doctrine to deter and, if necessary, defeat near-peer adversaries in a high-intensity conflict. This
concept has been formally incorporated into military doctrine with the publication of Joint Publication 1, Volume 1, Joint Warfighting, and serves as the foundation for
modernizing the Joint Force.
Concepts of Operations (CONOPS): Describe how the actions of the Joint force components and supporting organizations, consistent with ideas from Joint concepts,
are integrated, synchronized, and phased to accomplish a specific mission, war plan or function within the construct of a future scenario.  CONOPS support evaluation of
new ways of operating, future force posture mix, advanced capabilities, and authorities in exercises, wargames, and experiments.
