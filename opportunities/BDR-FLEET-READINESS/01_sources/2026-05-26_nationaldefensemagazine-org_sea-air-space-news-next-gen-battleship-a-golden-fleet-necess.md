## SEA-AIR-SPACE NEWS: Next-Gen Battleship a Golden Fleet Necessity, Phelan Says

Navy photo

**NATIONAL HARBOR, Maryland** — The next-generation Trump-class battleship is a necessity for U.S. naval forces to operate unconstrained by outdated platforms in a rapidly advancing warfighting environment, Secretary of the Navy John Phelan said April 21.

The Trump-class battleship, also known as BBG(X), announced by President Donald Trump in December replaces the service’s previous DDG(X) next-generation destroyer plans and anchors the Navy’s vision for the Golden Fleet initiative, Phelan said during a keynote speech at the Navy League's Sea-Air-Space conference.

“I’ve heard the critiques: too vulnerable, too expensive, too big,” Phelan said. “We’ve heard that before about carriers and about submarines — and yet, when it matters most, those are the platforms combatant commanders call for first.”

The Congressional Budget Office in January estimated that the first Trump-class battleship would cost between roughly $15 billion and $20 billion in fiscal year 2025 dollars. The high end of that cost would be closer to $22 billion if ordered in 2030.

Navy budget documents released April 21 expect the lead BBG(X) vessel to cost over $17 billion, with the first platform set for purchase in fiscal year 2028.

Many are asking, “Why battleships, and why now?” Phelan said. “The answer is straightforward and grounded in the realities of high-end conflict.”

“No commander should ever have to choose between air defense, anti-ship warfare, anti-submarine warfare or long-range strikes when defending our national interests and our greatest asset — our people,” he said.

The BBG(X) takes lessons learned from the DDG(X) program to enhance capability and design producibility, Phelan said. The ship will offer an array of modernized warfighting options, including network uncrewed systems, layered air and missile defenses, directed energy and high-speed, long-range strike.

“They are built to fight and stay in the fight by sustaining fires, maintaining pressure and outlasting any adversary,” Phelan said. These vessels will be “built not only to swat the arrows, but to kill the archer.”

The Trump-class battleship will be just one piece of the larger Golden Fleet picture, Phelan said. It may be the centerpiece of the future fleet, but “high-end capability alone does not win wars,” and the Navy must strike a high-low mix.

“A high-low mix secures control of the seas, creating the conditions to project power ashore,” Phelan said.

Crewed and uncrewed systems which operate alongside each other and interchangeably will optimize strength to win wars, he added. As the nature and scope of war evolves and expands, so must the sea services.

“While the United States Navy and Marine Corps remain the preeminent maritime force on the planet, our advantage is no longer uncontested,” he said, describing Trump’s plan for the Golden Fleet as not a mere aspiration, but the framework for achieving dominance at sea.

The White House’s fiscal year 2027 budget request asks for $65.8 billion toward Navy shipbuilding — “that’s 46 percent higher than fiscal year [20]26 and 123 percent more than fiscal year [20]25,” Phelan said.

The 2027 budget must be passed on schedule and in full, he said. Congress passing a continuing resolution instead would have “extremely negative consequences.”

“Continuing resolutions impose constrained short-term funding conditions that force legacy program trade-offs and impact our ability to innovate, and therefore our readiness,” Phelan said.
