# CACI capability taxonomy

CACI organizes its public-facing capabilities under a top-navigation grouping called "What we do," with seven primary areas. This file is the canonical taxonomy and the entry point to per-area detail. Each area name is taken verbatim from `caci.com`'s top navigation as of 2026-05-31; per-area summary descriptions, sub-capabilities, named customers, and named products are quoted from each area's dedicated CACI page on the same date.

One pattern cuts across all seven areas: **artificial intelligence is listed as a sub-capability of every capability area**. CACI presents AI as a cross-cutting technology rather than a standalone capability area. Treat this as a CACI strategic positioning fact — when scoring opportunities, an AI-related notice can map to multiple capability areas and the operator should not assume AI alone narrows the area.

## Cross-area observations

> **Assessment.** Three patterns are visible across the seven areas after Session 1:
>
> 1. **AI is positioned as cross-cutting**, not standalone — present as a sub-capability of every area. Opportunities tagged "AI" will need additional discriminators (mission domain, customer, etc.) to map to a specific area.
> 2. **Customer-named-on-page distribution skews to Army and IC** — Army appears on five of seven pages, Intelligence Community on three. Navy appears explicitly only on Mission and Engineering Support (via NSWC). The "Air Force" mention on multiple pages is generic; no specific Air Force major command is named.
> 3. **Product/platform naming is uneven** — Space, Spectrum, Cyber, and C3I all name specific products (PALLAS, VORTYX, FADE, DCGS, DarkBlue, etc.). Enterprise IT names none. Digital Solutions names one (ASF). The product naming roughly tracks capability areas that are technology-platform-led versus service-led.
>
> These patterns inform the scoring layer: an "Army + C3I + AI" notice has multiple matches; a "Navy + Enterprise IT + no specific product" notice has fewer Layer-2 signals to anchor on and will rank lower on capability-match unless the operator-team filter adds confidence.
