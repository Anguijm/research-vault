# Growth signals

This file documents what CACI's executives have said is growth-relevant for investors, with explicit attention to which signals are time-sensitive (refresh quarterly) vs. structurally durable (refresh annually). The file's organization reflects the staleness gradient — current-state first, durable themes second, historical baseline last.

## §1 — Current budget and financial state (FY26 Q3 10-Q, period ending March 31, 2026)

This is the most recent CACI executive commentary available. The next refresh point is CACI's FY26 10-K (filing expected approximately August 2026).

### §1.1 — GFY26 budget environment as it actually played out

> **FACT.** Two government shutdowns occurred during GFY26: (a) Oct 1 - Nov 12, 2025 (full government); (b) Jan 30 - Feb 3, 2026 (partial). `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** "On February 3, 2026, President Trump signed five of the six remaining GFY26 full year appropriations bills, as well as a two-week CR for the Department of Homeland Security. The defense appropriations bill was passed, providing full year funding for the Department of Defense (DoD) with a topline of $838.7 billion, approximately $8.4 billion above the President's defense budget request for GFY26." `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** "On February 14, 2026, the CR funding the Department of Homeland Security ended, and the department entered a shutdown. While DHS currently remains in a shutdown, portions of the department's operations have continued due to funding from the OBBBA." `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** GFY26 DoD topline at $838.7B is approximately **$54B lower** than GFY25's $893B full-year-CR level (-6.0%) — though it is $8.4B above the President's PBR. The OBBBA's $156B defense supplemental remains available throughout GFY26 and beyond as reconciliation-funded direct spending. Combined defense funding (appropriations + supplemental) is still higher than GFY25 base, but the smooth +13% growth picture the FY25 10-K had projected did not materialize.

> **Assessment.** **Shutdown risk materialized — twice.** Customers operating through repeated CRs and shutdowns experience delayed task-order awards, slipped program starts, and contract-action postponements. Expect FY26 pipeline volume to be patchy across quarters.

> **Assessment.** **DHS remains in partial shutdown as of the most recent 10-Q reporting.** This directly affects CACI's CBP / DHS-family task orders (including the 70RTAC vehicle from `vehicles.md` §5). Notices from DHS-family contracting offices may be delayed or canceled until the shutdown resolves.

### §1.2 — Q3 FY26 financials and backlog

> **FACT.** Q3 FY26 revenue: $2.351 billion, up 8.5% year-over-year. Operating income: $228.9 million (+16.6%). Net income: $130.4 million (+16.6%). Nine-month FY26 revenue: $6.86 billion. Organic growth: 6.8% (three months); 5.6% (nine months). `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** Total backlog as of March 31, 2026: **$33.4 billion** (up from $31.4B at end of FY25). Funded backlog: **$5.0 billion** (up from $4.2B). `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

### §1.4 — ARKA acquisition closed

> **FACT.** "On March 9, 2026, CACI acquired all of the equity interests of ARKA Group L.P. (ARKA) for purchase consideration of approximately $2,642.7 million, net of cash acquired, subject to post closing adjustments." `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** ARKA closed ~22 days before the quarter ended. Q3 FY26 financials include only partial-quarter ARKA contribution. Q4 FY26 (period ending June 30, 2026, reporting in August 2026) will be the first full-quarter ARKA-inclusive period.

### §1.5 — GFY27 PBR not referenced

> **Assessment.** The FY26 Q3 10-Q does NOT reference a GFY27 Presidential Budget Request. The PBR is conventionally submitted in February — about two months before this 10-Q was filed. Either the GFY27 PBR was delayed or it was filed too recently to incorporate. Recheck at FY26 10-K filing (~August 2026).

## §2 — Named market-trend priorities (structurally durable executive language)

The Market Environment section names eight market-trend themes CACI believes will drive U.S. government spending in CACI's addressable market over the next several years. The FY26 Q3 10-Q's eight trends are substantively identical to the FY25 10-K's, with slight reordering (AI moved earlier). This list is the most operationally useful artifact in the file because it maps directly to capability areas for scoring.

| # | Trend (verbatim) | Capability area mapping |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "A stable-to-higher U.S. government budget environment, particularly in national security-related areas (defense, intelligence, and border security)" | All seven areas |
| 2 | "Increased focus on cyber, space, and the electromagnetic spectrum as key domains for national security" | Cyber (§2), Space (§6), Spectrum Superiority (§7) |
| 3 | "Increased investments in advanced technologies (e.g., AI), particularly software-based technologies" | AI is cross-cutting (sub-capability of all 7 areas) |
| 4 | "Increased spending on network and application modernization and enhancements to cyber security posture" | Enterprise IT (§4), Digital Solutions (§3), Cyber (§2) |
| 5 | "Increasing focus on near-peer competitors and other nation state threats" | C3I (§1), Mission and Engineering Support (§5), Cyber (§2), Spectrum Superiority (§7) |
| 6 | "Increasing focus on application of technologies to defend the homeland" | Enterprise IT (§4), Cyber (§2) — CONUS-focused; less operator-team-relevant |
| 7 | "Continued focus on counterterrorism, counterintelligence, and counter proliferation as key U.S. security concerns" | Mission and Engineering Support (§5), Cyber (§2), C3I (§1) |
| 8 | "Increased demand for innovation and speed of delivery" | Cross-cutting — affects how all areas are delivered |

`[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]` (confirmed identical-in-substance with `[caci-10k-fy25-2026-05-31]`)

> **Assessment.** **Trend #5 ("near-peer competitors and nation-state threats") is the most operator-team-relevant signal.** The Indo-Pacific theater is the primary U.S. near-peer-competition AOR (China, North Korea, Russia in Pacific). Pacific Navy / Air Force opportunity work is in CACI's strategically prioritized geography.

> **Assessment.** **Trend #6 ("defend the homeland") is the only trend that's largely NOT operator-team-relevant.** Homeland defense work is CONUS-focused (border, infrastructure, FEMA-adjacent) and doesn't align with the Pacific operational theater. Score notices that lean homeland-defense lower for this operator unless the customer is a Pacific command.

> **Assessment.** Cyber, Space, and Spectrum Superiority are the three capability areas with explicit named-trend coverage in trend #2. These have the strongest documented growth tailwinds. Mission and Engineering Support and Enterprise IT are positioned via implication rather than explicit naming — suggesting these areas are stable rather than fast-growing in CACI's portfolio.

## §3 — M&A pace and capability addition pattern (durable structural signal)

> **FACT.** Seven acquisitions in three fiscal years (FY23-FY25): three in FY25, three in FY24, one in FY23. Plus ARKA closed in FY26 Q3 (§1.4 above) — making the rolling-3-year pace approximately 2-3 acquisitions per year. `[caci-10k-fy25-2026-05-31][caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** Net cash used in investing activities increased $1.6 billion in FY25 vs FY24, "primarily due to cash used in acquisitions." `[caci-10k-fy25-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** M&A is structural to CACI's capability expansion pattern. The operator-team should expect new capability areas to emerge between annual capability-book refreshes; the book needs M&A-driven update cadence (refresh `acquisitions.md` whenever a new acquisition is announced) beyond the quarterly refresh of this file. Pre-Session-1, the vault did not have `acquisitions.md` — now built and maintained.

## §4 — Risks and dampeners (durable structural signals)

> **FACT.** "Many of our federal government contracts require us to employ personnel with security clearances, specific levels of education, and specific past work experience. Depending on the level of clearance, security clearances can be difficult and time-consuming to obtain and competition for skilled personnel in the industry is intense." `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** "Changes in set-asides for small businesses and budgetary priorities" listed as risk factors. `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **FACT.** "Our customers' use of lowest price/technically acceptable (LPTA) procurements, which contributed to pricing pressures in past years, has moderated, though price still remains an important factor." `[caci-10q-fy26-q3-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** LPTA moderating favors CACI's "Expertise" differentiation framing over commodity pricing competition. For the operator, this means past-performance and capability narrative carry more weight in pursuits than they did when LPTA was dominant.

> **Assessment.** The FY25 10-K's risk-factor language about "the Department of Government Efficiency limiting, delaying, or reducing federal government spending" is NOT repeated in the FY26 Q3 10-Q. Either the risk has receded in CACI's executive framing, or DoGE-related disruption has been re-categorized into the "shutdowns and CRs" risk that did materialize. Worth re-checking in FY26 10-K.

## §5 — Historical baseline reference (FY25 10-K snapshot — for record only)

The FY25 10-K (filed August 2025) characterized the budget environment using a different snapshot than the current state in §1. **Do not use this for forward-looking interpretation.** It is preserved here only as a compact historical-record reference.

> **At time of FY25 10-K filing (Aug 2025):** GFY25 had been funded by a full-year CR signed Mar 15, 2025 at $893B defense — the first DoD full-year-CR. The GFY26 PBR submitted May 2, 2025 held defense flat at $893B. The OBBBA signed Jul 4, 2025 provided $156B defense supplemental (including $25B Golden Dome) and $170B border security. The 10-K projected combined GFY26 defense at approximately +13% over GFY25. **Actual outcome differed materially — see §1.1.** `[caci-10k-fy25-2026-05-31]`

> **FY25 full-year financials (annual baseline):** Total revenues $8.628B (+12.6% YoY), organic growth 7.2%. Net income $499.8M (+19.0%). Backlog $31.4B / funded $4.2B. Customer mix: DoD 75.4%, Federal Civilian 20.3%, Commercial 4.3%. Contract-type mix: cost-plus 60.5%, fixed-price 26.3%, T&M 13.2%. `[caci-10k-fy25-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment (FY25-baseline).** A 60%+ cost-plus mix is structurally important — cost-plus contracts are predominantly long-term IDIQs and program-of-record support, not short-cycle competitive bids. CACI's revenue base is anchored in steady-state program performance. Operator's AF IDIQ task order (FA807518D0006) fits this pattern. (This assessment remains valid in FY26 — the contract-type mix is not expected to shift materially quarter-over-quarter.)
