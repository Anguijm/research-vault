## §2 — Cross-references to capability areas

### Spectrum Superiority (§7)

> **FACT.** The Azure Summit acquisition adds "RF technology, ISR/EW/SIGINT/EMS" capability to CACI's Spectrum Superiority area. The capability page on caci.com explicitly attributes some SIGINT/EW/ISR technology to "Azure Summit acquisition." `[caci-azure-summit-acquisition-2026-05-31][caci-spectrum-superiority-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** Spectrum Superiority is now the area with the most identifiable acquisition-driven capability addition. The 300+ Azure Summit employees represent a material increase to the EW/SIGINT engineering workforce. Opportunity surfacing in Spectrum Superiority should account for this expanded capability when scoring.

### Enterprise IT (§4) and Digital Solutions (§3)

> **FACT.** The Applied Insight acquisition adds "cloud migration / adoption / transformation" capability with established DoD and IC customer relationships. `[caci-applied-insight-acquisition-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** Applied Insight's DoD+IC customer focus is operationally relevant to the operator-team because Pacific Navy / Air Force command IT modernization frequently involves cloud migration. The acquisition's customer relationships likely include Pacific-AOR offices but the press release does not specify; verify with future research if a Pacific opportunity surfaces requiring Applied Insight-style cloud-migration scope.

### Cyber (§2) and Mission and Engineering Support (§5)

> **FACT.** Bitweave Limited (UK FY23 acquisition) provides "software engineering, data analysis and cyber services to the national security sector" via CACI Limited (UK subsidiary). `[caci-bitweave-acquisition-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** Bitweave's UK organizational placement makes it primarily relevant to International Operations (3% of revenue per Session 2 business overview). Limited operator-team-Pacific relevance. Useful only as background for the multi-segment company structure.

### Digital Solutions (§3)

> **FACT.** Cyber-Duck (UK FY24 acquisition) provides "digital transformation agency specializing in UX and open-source technologies." `[caci-cyber-duck-acquisition-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** Same as Bitweave — UK organizational placement, primarily commercial UK customers. Limited operator-team-Pacific relevance.

### Space (§6) — forward-looking

> **FACT.** ARKA Group acquisition (announced 2025-12-22, $2.6B all-cash) will add "space-based sensor portfolio and ground-based software processing" to Space. `[caci-arka-acquisition-2026-05-31]`

> **Assessment.** ARKA is the largest CACI acquisition by deal size in this list, materially larger than Azure Summit ($1.275B). It will reshape the Space area in ways the FY25 10-K does not yet reflect. The next 10-K (FY26 filing in approximately August 2026) will document ARKA's contribution.

## §3 — Acquisition-pattern observations

> **Assessment.** Several patterns emerge from the five identified acquisitions:
>
> 1. **CACI uses M&A primarily to add Technology, not Expertise.** Azure Summit (RF), Applied Insight (cloud), ARKA (space sensors), Cyber-Duck (digital UX) all add technology platforms or product portfolios. Bitweave is the partial exception — adding services capability.
> 2. **UK acquisitions cluster in International Operations.** Both Bitweave and Cyber-Duck were acquired through CACI Limited, the UK subsidiary. CACI's UK M&A is segregated from US M&A, suggesting separate strategic tracks.
> 3. **Acquired companies are often portfolio companies of private-equity sellers.** Applied Insight was an Acacia Group portfolio company; ARKA is from Blackstone Tactical Opportunities. This pattern suggests CACI sources targets via PE-portfolio exit cycles, which has implications for the future M&A pipeline (PE holding-period exits are predictable).
> 4. **The two unidentified FY24 acquisitions are a research gap.** Closing this gap would require deeper press-release coverage of CACI's news section across calendar Q3 2023 - Q2 2024 (CACI FY24).
